PEOPLE: HINATUANONS

Some individuals think that there is a mixture of many ethnic groups for Hinatuan residents, including the Mandayas, Mansakas, Suban-ons, and Manobos. These ethnic groupings are of Malayan-Indonesian descent for Filipinos. Since 2020, the population has been greater or less than 40,000. Every guest is welcomed and treated kindly by the locals. They love being visited by visitors so they may start their enterprises and earn from them. Since they frequently interact with foreigners, most of them can communicate in English. They are known for their rich cultural heritage, warm hospitality, and strong community ties. The Hinatuanons often embrace a lifestyle deeply rooted in coastal traditions, fishing, agriculture, and celebrating local festivals that showcase their vibrant culture and customs. Their community thrives on the region's natural beauty, fostering a deep connection to the land and sea that surrounds them. Hinatuanons celebrates Paladong festival every year, it is an old tradition of the ethnic Kamayo. They performed dancing through tribal music, one of the spiritual belief for aid and guidance. Paladong is performed in response to a harmful event, like a mother losing her child.

The Paladong Festival typically showcases the town's cultural heritage through vibrant street dances, colorful costumes, and traditional performances. It is a commemoration of the town's history, traditions, and local folklore. The festival often features lively dance routines accompanied by rhythmic music, showcasing the unique cultural identity and artistic expressions of the Hinatuanons. The word "Paladong" is derived from the local dialect and refers to the traditional method of catching fish using a bamboo trap. This festival might highlight elements related to fishing practices or the significance of fishing in the community's history and livelihoods.
