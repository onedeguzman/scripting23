HINATUAN

GEOGRAPHY

Three significant rivers—the Hinatuan, Bigaan-Loyola, and Tagasaka-Tarusan rivers—cross the municipality. There is an abundant supply of forest products in this region simply due to its rivers, wonderful seacoast, and cold environment brought on by the mountain ranges of Baculin, Tagbobonga-Panlimoknan-Tagongon, and Tandawan. When you are traveling in Hinatuan, you'll discover the town is surrounded by mountains and trees, which creates a peaceful environment and leaves a sense of tranquility. Despite how challenging the elevated roads are to navigate, the scenery will leave you in awe of how stunning it is.

The municipality takes up 6.06% of Surigao del Sur's total area with a land area of 299.10 square kilometers, or 115.48 square miles. 43,841 people were living there as of the 2020 Census. This was equivalent to 1.56% of the total population of the Caraga area or 6.83% of the whole Surigao del Sur province. These numbers are used to calculate the population density, which comes out to 147 people per square kilometer or 380 people per square mile.

Hinatuan is politically subdivided into 24 barangays. Each barangay consists of districts:

  • Baculin
  • Bigaan
  • Cambatong
  • Campa
  • Dugmanon
  • Harip
  • La Casa (Poblacion)
  • Loyola
  • Maligaya
  • Bitoon (formerly pagtigi-an)
  • Pocto
  • Port Lamon
  • Roxas
  • San Juan
  • Sasa
  • Tagasaka
  • Tagbobonga
  • Talisay
  • Tarusan
  • Tidman
  • Tiwi
  • Zone I Benigno Aquino (Poblacion)
  • Zone II Santo Niño (Poblacion)
  • Zone III Maharlika (Poblacion)
